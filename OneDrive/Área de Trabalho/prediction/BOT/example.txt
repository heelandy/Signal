Broker/API route: Interactive Brokers can stream tick-by-tick Last, AllLast, BidAsk, and MidPoint, useful for basic live tick logic. (interactivebrokers.github.io)

Compute order-flow confirmation
For your system, the external engine should track:
bid/ask traded volume
cumulative delta
delta acceleration
absorption at demand/supply
stacked imbalance
sweep/rejection
DOM liquidity pulling/stacking
price movement sequence: long needs upward pressure starting, short needs downward pressure starting

For a long, outside Pine should confirm something like:
price breaks or reclaims your demand/supply level,
last ticks are moving up,
ask volume is beating bid volume,
cumulative delta is rising,
sellers fail to push price back under the level,
entry is not too far from the breakout/reclaim.
For a short:
price breaks down or rejects supply,
last ticks are moving down,
bid-side selling pressure is stronger,
cumulative delta is falling,
buyers fail to reclaim the level,
entry is not late in the move.
NinjaTrader’s Order Flow Cumulative Delta is exactly this type of tool: it compares volume filled at bid/ask or up/down ticks to measure buy/sell pressure. (ninjatrader.com) Sierra Chart Numbers Bars also exposes ask volume, bid volume, delta, cumulative delta, and per-price bid/ask differences. (sierrachart.com)
Important limitation: Pine cannot receive live external order-flow data back into the script in real time. So the clean architecture is:
Pine zones/context → webhook or duplicate logic → external tick/order-flow engine → alert/execution
If you want fastest practical route: use NinjaTrader or Sierra Chart first. If you want full custom automation: use Python/C# with Databento live data, compute the order-flow gates, then send alerts or orders through your broker.

Summary: the core goal is to know direction before firing any signal.
The script should not just detect a level. It must first decide if price is starting to move up, down, or is balanced/choppy.
Most important direction logic:
Long should only fire when price is beginning to push up.
Current candle bullish.
Close above previous close.
Previous close above the close before it.
Example movement: 101 → 102 → 103.
Price should be reclaiming demand or breaking/retesting supply with upward continuation.

Short should only fire when price is beginning to push down.
Current candle bearish.
Close below previous close.
Previous close below the close before it.
Example movement: 103 → 102 → 101.
Price should be rejecting/reclaiming supply or breaking down from demand with downward continuation.

No middle-of-trend signal.
If price already moved too far from the zone, do not chase.
Signal should happen near the beginning of the move, close to demand/supply, ORB edge, VWAP, EMA, or structure break.

No opposite-direction signal.
A long should not fire while price is actively going down.
A short should not fire while price is actively going up.

Direction should be checked continuously.
Chart timeframe direction gives stable confirmation.
1-second direction gives faster reaction.
Strict 1-second mode blocks signals unless the 1S direction agrees with the trade.

Pine limitation:
Pine can estimate live direction using 1S data, candle movement, VWAP, EMA slope, and volume pressure.
True “every tick” direction needs outside Pine order-flow/tick data.

Best rule for the system:
Zone setup + price movement direction + trend/regime confirmation + no chase = valid signal
So the signal is not only “price touched a zone.”
The signal is: price is reacting at the correct zone and starting to move in the trade direction.

Feature Engineering

Before training, raw data is transformed into numerical features.

Example calculations:

Body = abs(close - open)

UpperWick = high - max(open, close)

LowerWick = min(open, close) - low

Range = high - low

BodyRatio = Body / Range

VWAPDistance = close - vwap

EMASlope = ema50 - ema50_previous

ATRPercent = atr / close

Feature engineering is often more important than the choice of ML algorithm because it determines how much useful market information is available to the model.
